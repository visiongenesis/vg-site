**Vision Genesis is an owner-operated AI-implementation firm that pairs veteran operators with AI-native builders to solve the most expensive operating pain points inside real businesses — without replacing the people who run them.**

---

## 2. The thesis (why this exists)

The current AI services landscape has two failure modes:

1. **The AI-bro consultant** sells $50k strategy decks, hands a slide pile to the owner, and disappears. The owner can't operationalize it. Nothing ships.
2. **The dev shop** ships a tool nobody asked for. The owner pays $200k for software his team won't use because nobody embedded with operations long enough to know what the actual pain was.

Vision Genesis is the third path. We pair people who **ran real operations for 20 years** with people who **build AI-native systems**. The veteran knows what's actually killing the P&L. The builder ships the fix. The firm keeps building products from what we learn — so the next owner buys a packaged solution, not a custom project.

The bet: **most business owners don't need transformation; they need their three most expensive pain points solved.** We do that. Then we package what we learn.

## 5. The structural model — Operators + Builders + Agents

VG runs on a three-role model:

**01 — Operators.** Veteran operators who ran real operations for decades. They hold the truth about what's actually breaking inside a business. They mentor the builders, lead client relationships, and qualify engagements.

**02 — Builders.** AI-native technologists who ship working systems for paying clients from week one. They hold equity in the firm. They progress under operator mentorship.

**03 — Agents.** An internal team of named AI agents (9 today, 170+ specs in pipeline) that handles research, drafting, scheduling, monitoring. Coordination layer, not decision layer. People still make the calls — agents multiply judgment, never lead it.

**Output:**

**04 — Practices.** Each client engagement is a practice. One operator + one builder + one agent team installed inside a real business. Each practice is the unit the firm scales by.

**05 — Products.** Every engagement is R&D for the next product. What we solve for one owner becomes a packaged system the next owner can buy. The firm scales by standing up more practices AND productizing what proves useful.

This is **the VG Stack 01-05.** It's how the firm is organized, and it's the numbered structural device used across the website and content.

## 9. What the website must do

The website is the firm's storefront. It serves **four jobs-to-be-done**:

### Job 1 — Convince an owner they should engage

The owner-operator landing experience is the primary job. They land, they recognize the firm sees their actual problem, they trust the people, they take a small first step (Audit, Brief subscription, or intake form).

**Success metric:** owner takes ANY action in their first session — Audit signup, $47 Brief subscription, or intake form.

### Job 2 — Recruit operators and builders

Operator partners and builders evaluate VG as a place to plant their next decade. They need to see: equity model, the practice unit, the people, the existing infrastructure (9 agents, 170+ specs), the proof we ship.

**Success metric:** quality applications from veteran operators AND AI-native builders.

### Job 3 — Serve as the lookbook for content/social

Every YouTube thumbnail, IG post, X post, LinkedIn post should resolve back to the website. The site must visually rhyme with the social content (Apple Blue + Pantone 151 + Paper). When a viewer clicks through from Christian's video, the site must FEEL like the video — same palette, same Ryan, same numbered Stack.

**Success metric:** content → site click-through that doesn't bounce.

### Job 4 — Stand as the firm's living document

The principles, the Hard Rule, the Stack, the Tree — these are the firm's operating constitution. The site is where they live publicly. When a prospect, partner, or vendor asks "what do you actually stand for," the site is the answer.

**Success metric:** the site can be linked verbatim in response to that question, and the linker is proud to share it.

## 11. What the website must NOT do

- **Don't overclaim.** Refrain B ("we install operating systems") is forbidden on the storefront. Truth before sales pressure — Principle 2.
- **Don't lead with scale we don't have.** No "we've helped 500 businesses." We've helped a few. Say so honestly through the proof strip and product list.
- **Don't read as a literary magazine.** v6 had this problem. Paper + burgundy alone reads "wine label." The v8 palette (App Store Blue primary + Pantone 151 accent) solves it by adding tech-forward saturation.
- **Don't read as an AI-bro consultancy.** No dark-mode-on-electric-purple. No glassmorphism. No gradient mesh. No stock "AI handshake" imagery.
- **Don't use stock photography.** Ryan portrait in the locked crewneck OR HeyGen avatar render in the same outfit. That's it.
- **Don't change the locked taglines or principles.** They're R-approved across multiple rounds and are the firm's operating constitution.
