## The structural decision (locked)

> **Vision Genesis is a partnership. Each engagement is a practice.**

The firm is a partnership in the **Edward Jones** sense: broadly-held equity, clear operator authority, no co-op voting. The unit of work is a practice in the medical/legal/management-consulting sense: an owner-led pairing of one experienced operator and one AI-native architect, multiplied by an internal agent swarm.

**Why this language won (5 of 7 substantive votes Round 1, 5 explicit consensus Round 2):**
- "Partnership" carries the gravitas serious CEOs respect, without the Big-4 billable-hour pyramid (we'll disambiguate that in copy).
- "Practice" reads as serious craft — repeatable, owner-led, scalable across many engagements.
- Together they solve R's two hardest constraints: architects-as-owners (structural fact, not perk) and scale ambition (more partners → more practices, no boutique ceiling).
- "Pod," "Workshop," "Foundry," "Studio" all read smaller, more artisanal, or more juvenile than what VG is.

**Two-line elevator description:**
> Vision Genesis is an owner-operated partnership of experienced business operators and AI-native architects.
> We install AI operating systems through practices that multiply capacity at scale.

## The Seven Principles

The credo. Each principle is one declarative sentence with a one-line rationale. These are the philosophy of the firm and should appear on a dedicated **Principles** page (could replace or sit alongside "Field Notes").

**Page headline candidates:**
- *"Restructure the work. Multiply the people."* (GPT-5.5)
- *"Multiply the people. Restructure the work."* (people-first variant)

### 1. Restructure the work; never replace the people.

*AI multiplies team capacity. It does not reduce headcount. We do not serve companies whose explicit goal is to cut staff — that is the moral floor, drawn from Imago Dei.*

### 2. Builders are owners. Equity follows delivery, not tenure.

*Architects who deliver client systems hold equity in the firm. Ownership is not a perk; it is the structural fact of how the firm is held together. You earn it by shipping, not by waiting.*

### 3. Operators hold the truth when systems resist it.

*Twenty years of operating experience means knowing when the org chart is lying about what's actually happening. Every practice begins with an operator who has stood inside the room when the truth was inconvenient and named it anyway.*

### 4. Apprenticeship happens inside real client problems, not practice tracks.

*The young architects in this firm learn by shipping for paying CEOs under the eye of someone who has run real operations. There is no separate training program, no toy data, no demo environments. The work is the apprenticeship.*

### 5. Agents multiply judgment; they do not lead it.

*The internal agent swarm handles research, drafting, scheduling, monitoring, and follow-through. It does not make engagement decisions. Every recommendation that reaches a client traces back to a named operator and a named architect.*

### 6. Every claim is labeled Confirmed, Designed, or Unknown.

*Confirmed means we have shipped it and verified it. Designed means we have built it and not yet proven it. Unknown means we don't yet know. Clients deserve clean separation between fact, intent, and uncertainty.*

### 7. The firm is built by what it sells.

*Every consulting engagement is R&D for the next product. Solutions that work in one company become packaged systems for the next hundred. The firm scales through repeatable installations, not through hiring armies.*

## "How we work" — three paragraphs, ready to ship

> Vision Genesis is a partnership, not a consultancy pyramid. The architects who deliver client work hold equity in the firm. The experienced operators set the standard for what is true inside a business and what is theater. There are no junior staff, no billable-hour budgets to fill, no consensus rituals to slow the work down.
>
> Each engagement is a practice. One operator who has run real operations at scale. One AI-native architect who builds and ships at speed. An internal agent swarm that handles research, drafting, scheduling, and monitoring so a two-person practice delivers the output of a traditional consulting team. The agents do not make decisions. They give the practice room to think.
>
> The model scales because every engagement produces operating knowledge we can use again. What we install in one $20M company becomes a template for the next twenty. What we package as a product becomes ongoing software for the next hundred. The firm grows by standing up more practices and shipping more products — not by hiring armies of analysts.

## Risks the board flagged (manage in copy)

1. **"Partnership" reads as Big-4 billable-hour pyramid.** → First sentence of "How we work" pre-empts: *"Vision Genesis is a partnership, not a consultancy pyramid."*
2. **Architects misread equity as a perk or lottery ticket.** → Architects page line 2: *"Ownership here is a structural fact, not a recruiting perk."* Make it concrete with delivery-vested equity, not tenure-based.
3. **"Practice" reads generic if not earned.** → Every page needs operator + architect + named system to anchor the word.
4. **Mid-market CEOs default to billable-hour mental model.** → Engage page leads with fixed-scope pricing principles.
5. **FAANG/YC competition for architect attention.** → Architects page directly contrasts: not unicorn-or-bust, not maintaining old systems.
