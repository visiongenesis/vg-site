How It Works

Four phases. Named deliverables.
No mystery about what you’re buying.

Every engagement starts with a Bottleneck Unlock — a structured diagnostic that maps your operation, surfaces your highest-value opportunities, and tells you exactly where to start. From there, you know what we’re building, when, and what you end up with.

01

Diagnose

Week 1 · Free

Start with the free Bottleneck Unlock and a 45-minute discovery call. We map your operation, surface where time and money are bleeding, and rank your AI opportunities.

Output:A ranked list of your top three AI opportunities — and a go / no-go recommendation.

Your time:~30 min total (assessment + discovery call)

02

Scope

Week 2 · 1-week sprint

We build the implementation plan against the highest-leverage opportunity. You choose your engagement model: Advisory (you run the build, we coach), or Managed Implementation (we run the build end-to-end).

Output:Build plan, timeline, ROI model, and investment range. Engagement model chosen.

Your time:~1 hour (scope review session)

03

Install

Weeks 3–6 · 2–6 weeks

We deploy the AI system inside your stack, trained on your data, integrated with the tools your team already uses. Built by an Operator + Builder pair — never a hands-off engagement.

Output:A working AI system, deployed, tested, and ready to run.

Your time:~2 hrs / week (clarification access + 1 demo midpoint)

04

Teach

Weeks 7+ · Ongoing

We hand off a documented playbook, train your team, and monitor for the first 30 days. We stay available for refinement as your business grows. Most engagements step down to a monthly check-in by Week 12 — but the door does not close.

Output:Playbook in your hands, team trained, 30 days of monitoring, ongoing refinement.

Your time:~1 hour for playbook handoff; optional after

Integration

We build on your existing stack. We work with HubSpot, Salesforce, Slack, Google Workspace, QuickBooks, Notion, Airtable, n8n, Zapier, and the platforms your team already uses.

The Founder

Ryan Dix

Three founder ventures · Two Fortune-50 careers · One operator pattern

I spent nearly 20 years running large-scale finance operations at two best-in-class companies. ExxonMobil. Iron Mountain. I’ve built teams, led transformations, managed through crises, and helped shape decisions that mattered — to the bottom line and the humans that stewarded it.

What’s less obvious from the polished bio is the bench underneath it. Before the Fortune-50 work, I built and ran my own things. An e-commerce platform for four years. A restaurant I owned for two. One of those is on the wall as a credential. The other folded — as many restaurants do. I keep them both. The pattern recognition you build watching a business actually live or die is different from the pattern recognition you build inside an enterprise.

AI is under-hyped. The change is happening faster than most of us think. Late last year I started building Vision Genesis — a fully autonomous AI agent system, grounded in Human First ethics, built on local infrastructure. Not a demo. Not a prototype. A live, operating system. Specialized agents assessing and developing market strategies, iterating on product and service offerings, generating outreach, and co-building an AI management and advisory firm. Running 24/7.

I didn’t build it for fun. I built it with a conviction: AI should make people more capable, not obsolete. And the AIs that assist us should be trained on human-first ethics. We use this framework to design and deploy AI for clients — built for their specific operation, their pain points, and their opportunities.
