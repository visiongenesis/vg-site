The Founder

Ryan Dix

Three founder ventures · Two Fortune-50 careers · One operator pattern

I spent nearly 20 years running large-scale finance operations at two best-in-class companies. ExxonMobil. Iron Mountain. I’ve built teams, led transformations, managed through crises, and helped shape decisions that mattered — to the bottom line and the humans that stewarded it.

What’s less obvious from the polished bio is the bench underneath it. Before the Fortune-50 work, I built and ran my own things. An e-commerce platform for four years. A restaurant I owned for two. One of those is on the wall as a credential. The other folded — as many restaurants do. I keep them both. The pattern recognition you build watching a business actually live or die is different from the pattern recognition you build inside an enterprise.

AI is under-hyped. The change is happening faster than most of us think. In January I started building Vision Genesis — a fully autonomous AI agent system, grounded in Human First ethics, built on local infrastructure. Not a demo. Not a prototype. A live, operating system. Specialized agents assessing and developing market strategies, iterating on product and service offerings, generating outreach, and co-building an AI management and advisory firm. Running 24/7.

I didn’t build it for fun. I built it with a conviction: AI should make people more capable, not obsolete. And the AIs that assist us should be trained on human-first ethics. We use this framework to design and deploy AI for clients — built for their specific operation, their pain points, and their opportunities.

The problem we found

Social media isn’t optional anymore. It’s the primary advertising channel, the primary distribution channel, and increasingly the primary client-intake funnel. For an AI firm pitching SMB owners, showing up three times a week on YouTube and LinkedIn isn’t a nice-to-have. It’s table stakes.

The math: producing three educational episodes a week — AI Game Tape / Workflow ER / Business CSI — would have consumed roughly 25 hours of founder time every week. Not just on-camera. The full production stack. That’s 25 hours out of the field, out of client work, where the operator judgment is the product. The math doesn’t work.

What we built

Ryan Prime is an AI clone of Ryan Dix, built on a stack that didn’t exist as a stack two years ago. The script is still written by Ryan. The voice, the worldview, the judgment — all real Ryan. What Ryan Prime replaces is production: on-camera time, b-roll capture, editing wrapper. Same script in, polished episode out.

01

Diagnose

Week 2 · 1-week sprint

03

Weeks 3–6 · 2–6 weeks

We deploy the AI system inside your stack, trained on your data, integrated with the tools your team already uses. Built by an Operator + Builder pair — never a hands-off engagement.

Output:A working AI system, deployed, tested, and ready to run.

Your time:~2 hrs / week (clarification access + 1 demo midpoint)

Operate

Weeks 7+ · Ongoing

We hand off a documented playbook, train your team, and monitor for the first 30 days. We stay available on retainer for refinement as your business grows. Most engagements step down to a monthly check-in by Week 12 — but the door does not close.

Output:Playbook in your hands, team trained, 30 days of monitoring, retainer-based refinement.
